The time difference between Earth and Miller's Planet is about 1 Miller hour = 7 Earth years. Time dilation refers to the difference in the elapsed time measured by two observers, due to a relative velocity difference between them or by being differently situated relative to a gravitational field. In the context of planets, the difference in gravitational potential at different points in a gravitational field can lead to differences in the passage of time. For example, time passes slower for an object closer to a massive planet than for an object farther away, due to the stronger gravitational pull experienced by the former. This effect is known as gravitational time dilation and is a consequence of Einstein's theory of general relativity.
